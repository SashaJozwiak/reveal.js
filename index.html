What is SCRUM?

This is a project management methodology that is built on the principles of time management.

Its main feature is the involvement in the process of all participants, and each participant has his own specific role.

Who is who in SCRUM?

Product owner — someone who understands how this product should look / work. This person does not work in a team, he acts on the side. And this is the person who prioritizes tasks.

Scrum-master — Conducts Scrum meetings Enforces all Scrum principles, resolves conflicts, and shields the team from distractions.

Scrum-team — a team of project developers, consisting of specialists of different profiles.

What is what in SCRUM

Backlog — it is a list of all the work required to implement the product.

There are 2 types of backlogs: Product Backlog and Sprint Backlog.

Product Backlog — this is a complete list of all the work, the implementation of which we will get the final product.

Sprint Backlog — this is a list of work that the team has identified and agreed with the Product Owner for the next reporting period (sprint). Tasks in the sprint backlog are taken from the product backlog.

Sprint — a period of time (2-4 weeks) that is taken to complete a limited list of tasks (Sprint backlog), which are unchanged during the sprint, must be completed on time and demonstrated to the Product Owner.

Sprint planning — this is a meeting where everyone is present (team, scrum master, product owner). During this meeting, the Product Owner prioritizes the tasks they would like to see completed at the end of the sprint. The team evaluates over time how much of the desired they can accomplish. The result is a list of tasks that cannot change during the sprint and must be fully completed by the end of the sprint.

What first?

The product owner compiles the project backlog — this is a list of requirements for functionality, ordered by their degree of importance, to be implemented. The items in this list are called backlog items.

The project backlog is open for editing for all participants in the scrum process. The entire project backlog is divided into sprints for each of which a sprint backlog is formed.

Sprint Planning Meeting

— Occurs at the start of a new Sprint iteration;

— The tasks are selected from the backlog of the project, the obligations for the implementation of which for the sprint are assumed by the team;

— Based on the selected tasks, a sprint backlog is created. Each task is estimated in ideal man-hours;

— The solution of the problem should not take more than twelve hours or one day. If necessary, the task is divided into subtasks;

— Discussion and determination of how this scope of work will be implemented;

Daily Scrum meeting

— Lasts no more than 15 minutes;

— It is held in the same place during the sprint;

— During the meeting, each team member answers 3 questions: What have I done, what will I do, what are the difficulties and obstacles? The Scrum Master is working on solving these problems;

Sprint review meeting

— Held at the end of the sprint;

— The team demonstrates the increase in product functionality to all stakeholders;

— Attracts the maximum number of spectators;

— All team members participate in a demonstration (one person per demonstration or each showing what they did in a sprint);

— You may not demonstrate unfinished functionality;

— Limited to four hours depending on the length of the iteration and incremental product functionality;

If multiple commands work (Scrum of Scrums)

To be held after the daily Scrum meeting. Allows multiple Scrum Teams to discuss work, focusing on common areas and mutual integration. The agenda is the same as the Daily Scrum meeting plus the following questions:

— What has each team done since the previous daily meeting

— What each team will do for the next daily meeting

— Are there any issues hindering or slowing down the work of each team?

— Does the other team need to do something from your team's tasks?
